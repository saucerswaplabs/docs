Security is a top priority for SaucerSwap. To encourage responsible disclosure of vulnerabilities, SaucerSwap Labs offers bug bounty rewards based on severity, exploitability, and the quality of the report.

## V3 Testnet Bug Bounty

<Info>
  The SaucerSwap V3 testnet bug bounty is live now and is scheduled to close on **Monday, June 1, 2026 at 17:00 UTC**. SaucerSwap Labs may extend the program if the volume or quality of valid reports justifies a longer review period.

The V3 testnet bounty is the final layer of public review before V3 reaches mainnet. Builders, security researchers, and community members are invited to stress-test the V3 Central Limit Order Book and related V3 testnet surfaces.

### Testnet Setup

You will need a Hedera Testnet account to participate. You can create one directly in the SaucerSwap Mobile App or through the [Hedera Developer Portal faucet](https://portal.hedera.com/faucet).

Use the [SaucerSwap testnet interface](https://testnet.saucerswap.finance/) to test V3 functionality.

### Rewards

V3 testnet submissions will be classified across five severity levels based on the [OWASP risk rating methodology](https://owasp.org/www-community/OWASP_Risk_Rating_Methodology):

| Severity | Maximum reward |
| --- | --- |
| Critical | Up to $50,000 equivalent in SAUCE |
| High | Up to $10,000 equivalent in SAUCE |
| Medium | Up to $1,000 equivalent in SAUCE |
| Low | Up to $100 equivalent in SAUCE |
| Informational | Up to $25 equivalent in SAUCE |

SaucerSwap Labs will determine final reward amounts based on each bug's severity, exploitability, potential impact, report quality, and whether the issue is unique and previously unknown.

### V3 In Scope

- V3 testnet smart contracts
- V3 testnet interface
- Relevant V3 GitHub repositories
- SaucerSwap Mobile App surfaces related to V3, where applicable

### V3 Out of Scope

- SaucerSwap mainnet contracts and production environment
- V1 and V2 contracts
- SaucerSwap testnet interface sections that do not pertain to V3
- Third-party contracts or applications not directly associated with SaucerSwap
- Known issues from previous audit and bug bounty reports
- Known issues already identified during the current V3 security audit
- Any findings that rely on Denial of Service (DoS) or Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS)
- Phishing, social engineering, or attacks requiring collusion from SaucerSwap staff or third-party support
- Physical access, stolen/unlocked devices, SIM-swap, device-level malware, or OS/kernel exploits unrelated to the app
- Repackaged or modified app builds, emulator-only issues, or jailbreak-only read-only file access, unless chained to unauthorized signing
- Vulnerabilities in third-party libraries without demonstrable impact on SaucerSwap V3 or related SaucerSwap Mobile surfaces

### Reporting Requirements

Each submission should include:

- Conditions required to reproduce the issue
- Step-by-step instructions or a proof of concept
- A clear description of the potential impact if exploited
- Suggested remediation, if applicable
- Links to supporting evidence such as screenshots, recordings, logs, transaction IDs, repositories, or gists, if relevant

The more detailed and reproducible the report, the faster SaucerSwap Labs can validate and reward it.

<Warning>
  Do not disclose any identified vulnerability publicly until it has been resolved and disclosure has been explicitly approved by SaucerSwap Labs.
</Warning>

## Ongoing Bug Bounty Program

Outside of the active V3 testnet bounty, the general SaucerSwap bug bounty program remains focused on responsible disclosure of vulnerabilities across eligible SaucerSwap testnet, repository, and mobile app surfaces.

### Scope

- SaucerSwap [testnet contracts](/developerx/contract-deployments#hedera-testnet) and [GitHub repositories](https://github.com/saucerswaplabs)
- SaucerSwap [testnet interface](https://testnet.saucerswap.finance/)
- SaucerSwap Mobile App (iOS & Android)

The following are not within the scope of the general program:

- SaucerSwap mainnet contracts and production environment (testing restricted to testnet, which mirrors mainnet)
- Third-party contracts not directly associated with SaucerSwap
- Known issues from previous audit and bug bounty reports
- Third-party applications using SaucerSwap contracts
- Any findings that rely on Denial of Service (DoS) or Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS)
- Phishing, social engineering, or attacks requiring collusion from SaucerSwap staff or third-party support
- Physical access, stolen/unlocked devices, SIM-swap, device-level malware, or OS/kernel exploits unrelated to the app
- Repackaged or modified app builds, emulator-only issues, or jailbreak-only read-only file access, unless chained to unauthorized signing
- Vulnerabilities in third-party libraries without demonstrable impact on SaucerSwap Mobile

### General Rewards

The general program uses a four-level severity scale, based on the [OWASP risk rating methodology](https://owasp.org/www-community/OWASP_Risk_Rating_Methodology).

- **Critical:** Issues that could impact numerous users and have serious reputational, legal, or financial implications. An example would be being able to lock contracts permanently or take funds from all users.
- **High:** Issues that impact individual users where exploitation would pose reputational, legal, or moderate financial risk to the user.
- **Medium:** The risk is relatively small and does not pose a threat to user funds.
- **Informational:** The issue does not pose an immediate risk but is relevant to security best practices.

SaucerSwap Labs will determine rewards based on the bug's severity and its potential for exploitation. Rewards may be disbursed in U.S. dollars, cryptocurrency, or a mix of both.

### Disclosure

Report general-program vulnerabilities to [dev@saucerswap.finance](mailto:dev@saucerswap.finance). An acknowledgment will be sent within two to three business days. Do not disclose the bug publicly until it is resolved and permitted by SaucerSwap Labs.

A detailed report of a vulnerability increases the likelihood of a reward and may increase the reward amount. Please provide as much information about the vulnerability as possible, including:

- Conditions required for reproducing the bug
- Step-by-step guide or proof of concept for reproduction
- Potential consequences if exploited
- Suggested remediation (optional)

Anyone who reports a unique, previously-unreported vulnerability that results in a change to the code or a configuration change and who keeps such vulnerability confidential until it has been resolved by our engineers will be financially rewarded.

## Eligibility

To be eligible for a reward under this program, you must meet the following conditions:

1. **Uniqueness:** Discover a previously unreported, non-public vulnerability that is not already known to our team and is within the scope of the program.
2. **First Disclosure:** Be the first to disclose the unique vulnerability through the required reporting channel, and adhere to the program's disclosure requirements.
3. **Detailed Reporting:** Provide comprehensive information that enables our engineers to reproduce and remedy the vulnerability.
4. **Non-Exploitation:** Do not exploit the vulnerability in any form, including publicizing it or seeking other forms of profit, except under this program.
5. **Non-Publicization:** Do not disclose the vulnerability to the public or any third party without our explicit approval.
6. **Ethical Conduct:** Make a good faith effort to prevent privacy violations, data destruction, service interruption, or any degradation of in-scope assets.
7. **Lawful Behavior:** Do not engage in any unlawful conduct during the disclosure process, such as making threats or demands.
8. **Age Requirement:** Must be at least 18 years of age. If younger, you may participate with the consent of a parent or guardian.
9. **Legal Compliance:** Cannot be subject to U.S. sanctions or reside in a U.S.-embargoed country.
10. **Non-Affiliation:** Cannot be a current or former employee, vendor, or contractor who contributed to the development of the affected code.
11. **Complete Compliance:** Must comply with all other eligibility requirements specified in this program.

By meeting these criteria, you become eligible for a reward under the SaucerSwap Bug Bounty Program.

## Other Terms

By submitting a report, you grant SaucerSwap Labs the rights necessary to validate and resolve the vulnerability. All reward decisions are at our sole discretion. The program's terms may be changed at any time.
